01 — Context

Art Along
the Route

Cycle Arts Festival is an outdoor, mixed-arts programme running across Renfrewshire's cycle routes — work commissioned and exhibited in the landscape itself, encountered through movement rather than through a gallery door. Artists from across disciplines respond to specific sites along the routes, and the infrastructure of cycling becomes the spine the whole thing hangs off.

I exhibited as part of SCOPE Collective, showing a series of ceramic vessels and smaller forms inside a tented structure positioned directly along the route. The context is completely different from a gallery or even a craft fair — people arrive on bikes, often mid-journey, and the work has to earn their attention without making demands on it.

Work installed in a tent on a cycle path. No white walls, no plinths. Just the work and whoever happens to stop.

The festival ran 12–14 September 2025, with studio support from Outer Spaces, Abbey House. It was the first time I'd shown work in an entirely outdoor, site-embedded setting — and the first time the ceramics had to function without any of the usual staging a gallery provides.

02 — The Work

Glaze &
Surface

The pieces shown at Cycle Arts drew on two years of glaze development — combinations layered to encourage variation, crystallisation, and spangling rather than a uniform, predictable finish. The forms were deliberately restrained. Quieter shapes put more pressure on the surface to do the work, and the glazes respond better when they're not competing with the silhouette.

All work was fired at high temperature — primarily Cone 9 reduction, with some Cone 6 pieces from flecked stoneware. The difference in atmosphere between electric and gas firing produces different outcomes from the same glaze recipe, which is part of why reduction firing still interests me — you're working with the kiln rather than against it.

Crystal Emerald spangling differently at Cone 9. The copper and the reduction doing something I hadn't planned for.
